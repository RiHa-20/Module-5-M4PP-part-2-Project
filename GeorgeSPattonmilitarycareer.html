George S. Patton's Military Career

George S. Patton spent a year at the Virginia Military Institute and then transferred to Wthe U.S. Military Academy in West Point, New York. He graduated West Point in 1909 after having to repeat his plebe year because of grade issues. Seeing his first taste of battle in 1915 when Pancho Villa, a Mexican revolutionary, invaded the border town of Columbus, New Mexico. In 1917 when the the U.S. entered World War I, Patton promoted to captain and went over to France. In 1932 Patton graduated with distinction from the Army War College. In April of 1941 he was promoted to temporary major general, then weeks later made commander, of the 2nd Armored Division.In the summer of 1944, Patton's Third Army made a dramatic sweep across northern France and by January 1945 they had reached the Greman frontier. He was removed of his command of the Third Army in October of 1945. His last command was of the Fifteenth U.S. Army and on December 9th, 1945, Patton was involved in a low-speed car accident that caused serious head and spine injuries, twelve days later he died.

The awards and medals George Patton recieved from the U.S.

During his time career Patton recieved many awards for his service, below are the list of awards and medals he recived throughout his career.

  1. American Defense Service Ribbon
  2. Bronze Star
  3. Distinguished Service Cross with One Bronze Oak Leaf Cluster
  4. Distinguished Service Medal with Two Bronze Oak Leaf Clusters
  5. Distinguished Service Medal (U.S. Navy)
  6. European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Ribbon with One Silver Star and Two Bronze Stars
  7. Legion of Merit
  8. Mexican Service Badge
  9. Purple Heart
  10. Silver Lifesaving Medal
  11. Silver Star with One Bronze Oak Leaf Cluster
  12. Victory Medal (WWII)
  13. Victory Medal with Four Bronze Stars (WWI)
  14. *Sons of the Revolution Medal

All the listed awards are just the one's that Patton recieved from the United States, Patton also received countless other awards, medals, medallions, badges,or pins from other countries.
